About Me

My passion for Computer Science began in 8th grade, when I started to study JavaScript in order to create 3-D games and Java for Minecraft plugins. When high school started, I began to take programming more seriously by focusing on the Java language. Even though I took AP Computer Science, most of my knowledge came from self studying. A large part of my knowledge of algorithms came from the USACO (United States of America Computing Olympiad) training portal, where I continously improved myself by solving challenging problems that required critical thinking and some mathematics. Now, I am learning full-stack web development through online courses, and am planning to learn more about Java and start learning Artificial Intelligence in the near future. In my free time, I love playing the piano, practicing ping pong and tennis, and spending time with my little brother.

Experience

HackDFW (February 16-17, 2019)

HackDFW was a 24 hour challenge in which teams of 5 developed software or hardware that solved a specific problem. My team developed a web application named Capital One Cares that allows teenagers to become financially literate in a consequence-free environment by letting them manage a virtual bank account; it also allows parents to advise and keep track of the teen's progress.

TAMUhack (January 26-27, 2019)

TAMUhack was a 24 hour challenge in which teams of 4 developed software or hardware that solved a specific problem. My team developed an android application named TheraTracker that connects patients with therapists. Patients can log in and input information for their therapist to see such as today's mood, medications taken, etc., while therapists can also log in and view each of their patients' data.
